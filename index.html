Concept: Rational numbers on the number line

A rational number is any number that can be written in the form p/q, where p and q are integers and q ≠ 0. We can show rational numbers on a number line like points.

Teach 1 / 9 – Rational numbers and fractions

We recall that any fraction p/q with integers p and q (q ≠ 0) is a rational number. We practise identifying numerator, denominator and sign.

Board – Explanation & examples
  • 3/5 is a rational number (p = 3, q = 5).
  • −2/7 is also a rational number (negative in numerator).
  • 0 can be written as 0/1, 0/5, 0/10 (q ≠ 0).

Teach 2 / 9 – Equivalent rational numbers

We revise how to generate equivalent rational numbers by multiplying or dividing numerator and denominator by the same non-zero integer.

Board – Explanation & examples

  • Multiplying 2/3 by 2/2 gives 4/6.

Teach 4 / 9 – Comparing rational numbers

We compare rational numbers by bringing them to a common denominator and then comparing numerators.

Board – Explanation & examples
  • Compare 2/3 and 3/4: common denominator 12 → 8/12 and 9/12.
  • 9/12 > 8/12, so 3/4 > 2/3.
  • For negative numbers, more negative means smaller.

Teach 5 / 9 – Addition of rational numbers (same denominator)

We add rational numbers with the same denominator by adding numerators and keeping the denominator same.

Board – Explanation & examples
  • 2/7 + 3/7 = (2 + 3)/7 = 5/7.
  • −1/5 + 3/5 = (−1 + 3)/5 = 2/5.
  • 4/9 + (−2/9) = (4 − 2)/9 = 2/9.

Teach 7 / 9 – Subtraction of rational numbers

We subtract rational numbers using addition of the additive inverse: a − b = a + (−b).

Board – Explanation & examples
  • 3/5 − 1/5 = 3/5 + (−1/5) = 2/5.
  • 1/2 − 1/3 = 1/2 + (−1/3) = 3/6 − 2/6 = 1/6.
  • −2/7 − 1/7 = −2/7 + (−1/7) = −3/7.

Teach 9 / 9 – Division of rational numbers

We divide rational numbers by multiplying with the reciprocal of the divisor, provided the divisor is not zero.

Board – Explanation & examples
  • (2/3) ÷ (4/5) = (2/3) × (5/4) = 10/12 = 5/6.
  • (−3/7) ÷ (1/2) = (−3/7) × (2/1) = −6/7.
  • Division by 0 is not defined.
